PART II. SOCIO-ECONOMIC, CULTURAL AND DEMOGRAPHIC BACKGROUND

Fortune High School was built in a vacant lot situated along Santan St., and located right just beside Fortune Elementary School.

SOCIO-CULTURAL

Marikina City (Filipino: Lungsod ng Marikina), located in the island of Luzon in the Philippines, is one of the cities that make up Metro Manila, the National Capita Region. Marikina shortly became a former capital of the Province of Manila during the declaration of Philippine Independence from 1898 to 1899.

Marikina was given the title Shoe Capital of the Philippines because of its notable shoe industry, being the biggest manufacturer of shoes in the Philippines, producing almost 70% of shoe production in the country. Marikina currently holds the World’s Largest Pair of Shoes and recognized by the Guinness World Records, also, the Shoe Museum houses part of the famous shoe collection of the former First lady Imelda Marcos, shoes of the world leaders, celebrities and shoes of different countries, making it the largest collection of pair of shoes in the world.

Marikina has its own share of attractions to offer. Sports and recreational facilities are available to its residents and those from nearby towns and cities. Historical landmarks and church, antiquated houses and galleries view to the attention of history and culture buffs. It’s also attracts major commercial centers, man-made parks, large structure buildings, and most of them, a traditional shoe-making in the city, the natural river parks and the hospitality of Marikeńos 

POLITICAL

Like in other city governments in the Philippines, Marikina is governed by a mayor and a vice mayor elected to three-year terms. The mayor is the executive head and leads the city’s departments in executing the city ordinances and improving public services along with city councilors while the vice mayor heads a legislative council along with barangay councilors. Every barangay also has its own set of projects, providing community service in the city as well as basic services provided for remote residences. It was also awarded numerous times in good governance and integrity. Marikina boasts one of the wealthiest local governments in the Philippines in terms of current assets, cash in banks, share of internal revenue allotment, gross income and gross net income.

Marikina is divided into 2 districts, Districts 1 and 2, where each district is represented by a congressman in the House of Representatives of the Philippines. Marikina consists of 16 barangays, where each barangay is headed by a barangay chairman along with their barangay councilors.

Every barangay has a police station that provides security and crime prevention in the area. Fire stations are also located in strategic areas to easily access in case of fire. CCTV cameras are also set in major intersections and busiest areas in the city to monitor traffic and crime events. There are public safety and security offices in the city, and its justice hall is beside the city hall. Rescue 161 is a hotline number that provides help within the city.

The south portion of the city occupies the numerous commercial, industrial and residential areas, heritage sites and mixed use zones, while the north and northeast portion are primarily for residential and industrial zones, and various establishments such as small and medium enterprises. Located along the eastern border of Metro Manila, it is bordered on the west by Quezon City, to the south by Pasig City and Cainta, Rizal, to the north by San Mateo, Rizal and to the east by Antipolo City, the capital of Rizal province. DEMOGRAPHY

Based on the National Statistics Office, the estimated population size of Marikina City is 496,205 while the household size is 105,351 and the average household size per barangay is 4.71. This population size is attributed to migration and population explosion.

ECONOMY

Marikina was the biggest manufacturer of quality shoes, and Marikina produced 80% of the shoe production in the Philippines. It is also the Philippines’ largest explorer of leather shoes throughout the world. All locally produced shoes are made in Marikina, from slippers to shoes. Marikina also produced shoes which is made of rubber , wood (bakya) and plastic. Marikina still the undisputed the Shoe Capital of the Philippines and even in Asia.

Marikina is the home to a member of industries, some of which are the biggest and most successful in their fields, and foremost of all, shoe manufacturing, being the best in local craftsmanship. Shoe and leather industry

still the top livelihood in the city and generates thousands of jobs within the city.

The city has a large number of internet shops, parlors, eateries, bakeries, made-to-order products such as food processing and delicacies, and franchising as their common income. The city provides job fairs, livelihood projects, free training and seminars for its people who want to start or upgrade their business. The city manufactures food processing, cigarettes, porcelain and chinaware, confectionery, automobiles and cars, appliances, electronics, crafts like bags, hats and slippers, and foremost of all shoes.



Public markets in Marikina are often divided into two, the dry goods section and the wet goods section. Commerce in these public markets are in full swing, especially in the early morning. Under the urban renewal program of the city, some of the public markets had been refurbished and given a fresher look, like the “Marikina Public Market” also known as “Marikina Market Mall”, or locally called the “Pamilihang Bayan”, the biggest and cleanest market in Metro Manila.

Modern shopping malls dot the city especially in the southern portion of Marikina. SM City Marikina, Blue Wave Mall at Marquinton, Robinsons Place, are some of Metro Manila’s major shopping and commercial centers stands along Marcos Highway. The popular commercial center that lies in Marikina is the Riverbanks Center.

For the adventurous shoppers, you may venture beyond the hotel or shopping complex package and combine other interesting destinations for cheap buys such as in Marikina Market Mall. Bargaining is the major part of your shopping experience when you pass by that area, as it sells goods at rock-bottom prices. Riverbanks Center gives a comfort to the shoppers as it offers air-conditioned mall, but the price of the goods here is still very similar to the goods bought outside. Some of goods are cheap electronic products, toys and other cheap equipments needed into your homes.

Almost all of the major commercial, international and local government banks in the Philippines operate a branch in the city. Major banks operate in the city with more than one branch. At this time, there are 60 banking institutions offer banking services to businesses and residents. Most of these are concentrated in Barangay Sta. Elena, Sto. Nińo, Concepcion Uno, San roque, calumpang and Barangka.

Marikina lined up as one of Information Technology hub in Metro Manila and continuously growth of outsourcing and call centers, among them is ICT Groups, Skyes, NCO, Teletech, to name a few. There are numerous contact centers worldwide with IT-based offices in the Philippines in Makati City, Ortigas Center and riverbanks Center in Marikina City. 

PART III. HISTORY

The rapid increase of student population in Marikina City and the continuous congestion of the existing secondary schools, the City Mayor Hon. Marides C. Fernando initiated the establishment of Fortune High School.

The school building was built in a vacant lot situated long Santan St., and located right just beside Fortune Elementary School.

In May 2007, the school was made as an annex of Marikina Heights High School through the efforts of Dr. Flordeliza R. Mayari, the Schools Division Superintendent.

The operation of the school started in June 20017, headed by the very dynamic Officer-In-Charge, MR. WILFREDO S. JABAL, who then faced a lot of challenges as the school was just starting. With 17 contractual teachers to teach a number of 1,030 students, they were able to start providing a quality education.

Fortune High School, on its early operation as an annex school has proven its worth when it was chosen as the host school for the observation of the former President, Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo on January 1, 2008.

The whole school year of 2007 – 2008 was a bit successful. Then, in June 2008, DR. LYDIA B. TIMOTEO, Principal III, took over to manage the school. Known for her accuracy, diligence and initiative in working out school’s status, Fortune High School was put to her trust.

As Dr. Lydia B. Timoteo moved to Fortune High school, permanent teachers from Marikina Heights High School were also transferred as one of the requirements of becoming an independent school. In just some time, the school attained its independence on August 12, 2008 through the collaboration of the local government, parents, teachers, and students for whom the building is wholeheartedly dedicated.

The school, young as it is, has not only proven its competitiveness, socially and academically through attending and winning some school contest and events but also through participating in contest held by the city. This was best proven during the Rehiyon-Rehiyon 2009 when Barangay Fortune and the school itself reigned as the champion on both the street dancing and dance participation.

The leadership of Dr. Lydia B. Timoteo has helped a lot with regards to the school’s physical facilities and staff development. The school was given 19 permanent items in addition to the nine items transferred to the school.

Driven by strong desire toward educational competence, Dr. Eduardo V. Lopez, Schools Division Superintendent, issued a reassignment of principals effective July 5, 2010. Thus, fortune High school has another leader in the name of MRS. NELLY C. CONDE, the principal of Tańong High School. Her legacy and achievements in Tańong High School will also be shared here in Fortune High School. She has already started some innovations for the improvement of the school particularly the academic performance of the students.

With the transition of a new Schools Division Superintendent, Dr. Elizabeth E. Quesada, the foundation of Fortune High School had matched up its current improvements and standards. In a matter of time, Fortune High school had indeed showed exemplary performance in various District. Division and even Regional Competitions.

Just recently, another transition occurred as Mrs. Nelly C. Conde went back to Tañong High School and Fortune High School welcomed its former school administrator, DR. LYDIA B. TIMOTEO. She is more than willing to regain her former plans for the holistic development of Fortune High School. She has been very active in strengthening the academic performance of students especially in the light of the National Achievement Test.

5D GOVERNANCE

1. School Management and Supervision

The school is managed and supervised using the school Improvement Plan (SIP) and the Annual Implementation Plan (AIP). All school projects and programs must be aligned and implemented based from the SIP and AIP. The school head together with the Head Teachers, Master Teachers, Year-Level Chairpersons, Department Heads and Guidance Counselor, disseminates vital information about new programs and projects to teachers, students and parents.

2. Parents’ and Community Participation

The school has a very supportive Parents-Teaches Association. They are key stakeholders in the proper implementation of school projects and programs through cash or in-kind donations and supplementary workforce. They are very active in participating to the annual Brigada Eskwela conducted before the opening of classes.

To ensure the best interest of the students, certain government and non-government organizations are tapped such as:

· Philippine Red Cross – Marikina City Branch of Rizal Chapter
· Boy Scouts and Girl Scouts of the Philippines
· Marikina City Junior Police
· Rotary Club of Marikina
· Supreme Students’ Government
· Youth Mary and Christ (YMC)
· Batang Iwas Droga Association (BIDA)
· Batang Emergency Response Team (BERT)
· Fortune Network Journalism Society
· Sports Organizations
· Tzu Chi Foundation
· Youth for Environment Students’ Organization (YES-O)
· Academic Clubs

3. Instructional Supervision

Annual supervisory plan is prepared every year as a guide in the supervision of teachers. 16 teachers are observed every month or as the need arise. Teachers are required to submit advance lesson plans in order to ensure their preparedness in everyday classroom teaching. Post conference was conducted using the Teaching-Learning Observation Checklist (TLOC).

4. Resource Mobilization/Public Expenditure

The educational services of the school, being a government academic institution had been chiefly by the national government through the MOOE Fund. Other financial needs of the school for its operational requirement had been provided by and/or taken from Local Government Units (City Government of Marikina, Barangay Council), Local School Board (FHS Faculty Club), FHS Consumer Cooperative funds donations, and PTA.
